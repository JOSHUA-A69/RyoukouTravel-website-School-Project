Panoramic Park Alicia,Bohol

Origin/History:Alicia Panoramic Park is located in Alicia, a town around 2.5 hours away from Tagbilaran City, Bohol. Its peak, Binabaje Hills, rises 400 meters above sea level and is covered in long cogon grass. The town is also where Alicia Schist, the oldest known rock in Bohol, can be found. These rocks are believed to have existed since the near end of the Jurrasic Era, which is the era of dinosaurs, to the early Cretaceous Era, around 145-100 Million years ago.

What to do:The entrance fee is 30 pesos, and you can also hire a local guide for 300 pesos for the day hike and can pay an additional 500 pesos if you plan to go camping with 5 people. Tents and trekking poles are available for rent as well. The duration of the hike can take about 1.5 hours to 4 hours depending on your pace.

Food to eat:Bringing your own food is a must since it is a hiking trail, but there are snacks and drinks for sale at the registration area, so make sure to buy what you need.

Transportation:Public transportation from Dao Integrated Bus Terminal takes more or less 3 hours via bus or van. A motorbike or private transport is ideal too since they have a wide parking space available.





Can-Umantad Falls Candijay,Bohol

Origin/History:Can-Umanted Falls is in the Candijay region (pronounced ‘Candee-high’). It was named after its height. Years ago, there was no road that went all the way around to Cam-Umantad Waterfall.

What to do:There is a rice terrace near the waterfall if you walk down the steps. It will take a 15-minute walk to the waterfall down the steps. The entrance fee is about 20 pesos. There are a lot of great spots, you can head on up to the main waterfall, which is the tallest waterfall in the province. The main cascade is 60ft but hits a plateau before dropping down to the main pool.

Food to eat:There is a restaurant near the waterfall. It's like a 2-in-1 attraction.

Transportation:There is a great tour that takes you from your hotel in Panglao to the beaches of Anda, Candapdapan Rice Terraces, and to Can-Umantad Waterfall. It is about a 2-hour journey from Panglao, arriving in the Candijay Region, it is actually not super easy to find with a number of dirt roads to follow.





Cadapdapan Rice Terraces Candijay,Bohol

Origin/History:The terraces were formed from elevated areas converted into farmlands by the natives of barangay Cadapdapn a long time ago. Usually seen in pictures, the terraces’ dominating color is golden yellow. A color that reminds them of the wealth left for them by their ancestors that is indeed worth remembering.

What to do:There is a hiking trail that you can follow, and also there are other spots you can also visit like the Can-Umantad Waterfall and Canawa Cold Spring. It’s a great region for a day trip because you have these three unique attractions all within close vicinity.

Food to eat:There is a restaurant in the middle of the terraces and you will need to pay a small entrance fee to pass through the gate.

Transportation:You can get there by public transport from Tagbilaran City for about 80 pesos. You can also get there with a Van for 130 pesos. It is open on weekdays (Monday-Friday): 8:00 Am to 5:00 Pm as well as Weekends.





Sea of Clouds Danao,Bohol

Origin/History:The Sea of Clouds, Bohol, is filled with an overcast layer of clouds sitting across rice terraces and hillsides.

What to do there:Enjoy the stunning scenery, take photos, hike around the area, and experience the sensation of being above the clouds. Bring a warm jacket and trousers if you are traveling on a scooter. It is very chilly in the morning. The clouds form between 5 am-7 am, however close to 7 am, most of the clouds will have disappeared.

Food to eat: Villa Del Carmen Haven Bohol is the closest option with a good food rating, still, it's an hour's drive away from the Sea of Clouds.

Transportation:It is accessible by road, and you are able to take private transportation. The journey from Panglao, Bohol Philippines to the Sea of Clouds takes about 2 hours by car or scooter. The entrance fee is about 10 pesos, you can enter with your scooter or vehicle and park it at the bottom of the viewing point.





Himontagon Hills Loay,Bohol

Origin/History:Himontagon Hills in Loay offers panoramic views of rolling hills and lush landscapes. Himontagon Hills, also referred to as Morning Hills, have been a favorite, go-to spot for locals who are longing to escape the clutches of their bustling modern-day life. Praised for its verdant hills and fresh mountain air, these hills boast a soothing feel that can absolutely take away all your stress.

What to do there:Hike or bike through the hills, enjoy the scenic views, take photos, and relax in the natural surroundings.

Food to eat:There might not be any food vendors nearby, so be sure to bring your own food and water.

Transportation:Accessible by road, with options for private transportation or guided tours.





ANDA

Origin/History:The Municipality of Anda is a 3rd class municipality in the province of Bohol, Philippines. It was formerly known as Quinale and was a barrio of the municipality of Guindulman from the early part of its civilization up to the later decades of the 19th century. It was basically uninhabited with plenty of uncultivated lands and its shores were swampy with many mangroves and hardwood trees. Now, however, Anda is famous for its beaches, and deep blue cave pools such as Combento and Cateres.

What to do:Relax on the pristine beaches, explore caves and rock formations, snorkel or dive in the crystal-clear waters, and enjoy water sports.

Food to eat:Sample fresh seafood dishes at local restaurants or beachside eateries.

Transportation:Accessible by road from Tagbilaran City or Panglao, with options for tricycles, motorcycles, or private cars for local transportation.





Jardin Necitas,Pilar Glowing Garden

Origin/History:Located in Pilar, is an outdoor garden that features 200,000 artificial flowers that glow with LED lights. Located in Purok 5 Brgy. Bagumbayan in Pilar, Bohol. The glowing grade is situated on a hilltop, where it has the requisite scenic pastoral views overlooking the mountain ranges of the town of Ubay and Mabini during the daytime.

What to do:Admire the illuminated displays, take photos, and enjoy the enchanting ambiance.

Food to eat:Some vendors may offer snacks or refreshments on-site, but bringing your own food is recommended.

Transportation:It is accessible by road, with options for private transportation or guided tours. The entrance fee is 30 pesos and the opening hours are from 10 am - 10 pm daily.





Combento Cave Anda,Bohol

Origin/History:Combento Cave, located in Anda, is just a few distance away from the provincial road, there lies another must-visit spot called Combento Cave. This cave, indeed is not your ordinary cave. It’s a cave pool. You can literally enjoy swimming inside. Though the whole chamber is not that big, the deepest part of the water measures more than 6 feet - enough to have your whole body in a standing position submerged. As to where the water came from, it remains a mystery. It seems it just springs out from the ground. The close proximity of the cave to the seashore might also be the reason why this cave has water in it.

What to do there:Explore the cave system, go spelunking, marvel at the natural formations, and learn about the cave’s geological history. Be there early in the morning when there are still a few tourists around; the place gets crowded throughout the day.

Food to eat:Food vendors might not be nearby. So best to bring your own food and drinks.

Transportation:Accessible by road, with options for tricycles or motorcycles for local transportation. The entrance fee is 20 pesos; no other charges to pay. From Tagbilaran Airport, take a tricycle ride to Dao Bus Terminal, then take a bus ride bound for Anda.





Sardine Run at NapalingDive Site Panglao,Bohol

Origin/History:Napaling Dive Site in Pamilacan Island is famous for its sardine run, where thousands of sardines move in synchronized patterns. Napaling Point, also called Napaling Reef, was once a secluded and off-the-beaten-path destination that even locals didn’t know how to get to.

What to do:Snorkel or dive to witness the mesmerizing sardine run, explore the vibrant marine life, and enjoy underwater photography. This place is particularly great for divers - for both scuba and freedivers. Several dive shops have sprung up in the last few years as the place gets more and more popular. This is a 6-hour tour that already comes with the entrance fee, the assistance of a tour guide, and air-conditioned van transfers. Life vests are included, and the use of snorkeling here also comes with this tour.

Food to eat:Bringing your own snacks and water is a must because there are no food vendors nearby.

Transportation:Accessible by boat from nearby coastal areas, with options for organized dive tours. You can schedule the tour to start as early as 6 am. If you are staying in a hotel or resort in Panglao Island or Tagbilaran





Loboc River

Origin/History:The Loboc River (also called Loay River) is a river in the Bohol Province of the Philippines. It is one of the major tourist destinations of Bohol. The source of the Loboc River is located in the town of Carmen, almost in the center of Bohol.

What to do:Take a river cruise, enjoy lunch or dinner at a floating restaurant, listen to live music, and admire the riverside scenery. The Loboc river passes through the town center of the Municipality of Loboc, offering a riverside view of the Loboc church across the highway. Various activity on the river, including standup paddleboarding, and enjoy other activities like cultural presentations, firefly watching, birding, and even paddleboard yoga.

Food to eat:Enjoy traditional Filipino dishes served in the floating restaurants, including seafood, grilled meat, and local delicacies.

Transportation:Accessible by road from Tagbilaran City or Panglao, with options for organized tours or private transportation.





Balicasag Marine Sanctuary

Origin/History: Balicasag Marine Sanctuary offers a pristine white sand beach and stunning coral reefs, making it a must-visit destination for sea lovers. The island is known for its rich marine life and is popular for activities like diving, snorkeling, island hopping, and dolphin watching.

What to do: Experience the wonders of Balicasag by diving into its crystal-clear waters, where you can encounter turtles, vibrant corals, and schools of fish. Don't miss the opportunity to explore the marine sanctuary's famous dive points, including The Black Forest, known for its abundant black corals and diverse marine species.

Food to eat: Enjoy a taste of local cuisine at nearby eateries, offering fresh seafood and traditional Filipino dishes.

Transportation: Access Balicasag Island via boat from Panglao Island, with dolphin watching tours typically departing early in the morning.





San Isidro Beach

Origin/History: San Isidro Beach is a tranquil stretch of coastline located in Bohol, known for its serene ambiance and picturesque surroundings. The beach offers a relaxing escape for visitors seeking a quiet retreat away from the bustling tourist areas.

What to do: At San Isidro Beach, you can unwind on the soft sands, take leisurely strolls along the shore, and bask in the beauty of the natural scenery. The calm waters make it ideal for swimming and sunbathing, while the gentle breeze provides a refreshing respite from the heat.

Food to eat: While there are limited dining options directly on San Isidro Beach, visitors can pack a picnic and enjoy a meal amidst the peaceful seaside setting. Alternatively, nearby restaurants offer delicious seafood dishes and local delicacies.

Transportation: San Isidro Beach is accessible by land transportation from various points in Bohol. Visitors can rent a car or hire a tricycle or motorcycle taxi to reach the beach area. Additionally, organized tours may include San Isidro Beach as part of their itinerary, providing convenient transportation for tourists.





Hinagdanan Cave

Origin/History: Hinagdanan Cave is a natural wonder located in Dauis, Panglao Island, Bohol. The cave's name, "Hinagdanan," is derived from the local dialect, which means "laddered" or "descended." It was discovered by locals who stumbled upon its entrance hidden amidst lush vegetation.

What to do: Visitors to Hinagdanan Cave can explore its fascinating limestone formations, stalactites, and stalagmites, which have formed over thousands of years. Inside the cave, you can marvel at the intricate patterns and textures created by nature's geological processes. Adventurous souls can take a refreshing dip in the cave's underground pool, which is fed by natural springs and illuminated by sunlight streaming through openings in the cave ceiling.

Food to eat: While there are no food establishments within the cave itself, visitors can enjoy a meal or snacks at nearby eateries and stalls offering local delicacies and refreshments.

Transportation: Hinagdanan Cave is easily accessible by land transportation from various points in Panglao Island and other parts of Bohol. Visitors can rent a car or motorcycle or hire a tricycle taxi to reach the cave entrance. Additionally, guided tours to Hinagdanan Cave are available, providing transportation and guided exploration of the cave's wonders.





Dimaio’s twin waterfalls

Origin/History: Dimaio’s Twin Waterfalls, nestled in the heart of Dimiao, Bohol, is a natural gem hidden within the lush tropical landscape of the province. The waterfalls derive their name from the local term "twin," signifying the presence of two distinct cascades that converge into a serene pool below. The site remains a favorite among locals and tourists seeking tranquility and natural beauty.

What to do: Visitors to Dimaio’s Twin Waterfalls can enjoy a scenic hike through verdant forests and picturesque landscapes to reach the cascades. Upon arrival, travelers can immerse themselves in the refreshing waters of the twin falls, taking in the breathtaking sights and sounds of nature. Adventurous souls can also engage in activities such as cliff diving, swimming, and exploring the surrounding jungle trails.

Food to eat: While there are no food stalls or eateries directly at Dimaio’s Twin Waterfalls, visitors can pack a picnic lunch or snacks to enjoy amidst the natural beauty of the area. Additionally, nearby towns and villages offer a variety of dining options where visitors can savor local delicacies and refreshments.

Transportation: Dimaio’s Twin Waterfalls is accessible by land transportation from major towns and cities in Bohol. Travelers can rent a car or motorcycle, hire a local guide, or take a guided tour to reach the waterfalls. The journey typically involves navigating winding roads and scenic routes, providing travelers with an opportunity to appreciate Bohol's diverse landscapes along the way.





Danao Adventure Park

Origin/History: Danao Adventure Park, situated in the municipality of Danao City, Bohol, is renowned as the "Extreme Adventure Capital of Bohol." Established to promote eco-tourism and adventure sports in the region, the park offers a wide array of thrilling activities amidst the scenic backdrop of Danao's lush landscapes and rugged terrain. Originally conceived as a community-based sustainable tourism project, Danao Adventure Park has evolved into a premier destination for adrenaline-seekers and nature enthusiasts alike.

What to do: At Danao Adventure Park, visitors can partake in an exhilarating range of outdoor adventures and adrenaline-pumping activities. From zipline rides that soar above the treetops to canyon swings that plummet into lush ravines, the park caters to thrill-seekers of all ages and preferences. Other popular activities include rappelling down sheer cliffs, kayaking along winding rivers, and exploring scenic nature trails on foot or mountain bike. For those seeking a more leisurely experience, the park offers opportunities for birdwatching, picnicking, and enjoying panoramic views of the surrounding countryside.

Food to eat: Danao Adventure Park features on-site dining facilities where visitors can savor delicious meals and refreshments amidst the park's natural surroundings. The park's restaurant offers a variety of local and international cuisine, including freshly prepared seafood dishes, grilled meats, and vegetarian options. Additionally, visitors can sample refreshing beverages and tropical fruit juices to recharge after a day of adventure.

Transportation: Danao Adventure Park is accessible by both public and private transportation from major towns and cities in Bohol. Travelers can reach the park via land transportation, with options including buses, vans, and rented vehicles. The park is located approximately 72 kilometers northeast of Tagbilaran City, the provincial capital of Bohol, and can be reached via a scenic road trip that traverses the island's picturesque countryside.





Panglao Island

Origin/History: Panglao Island, located southwest of the main island of Bohol, is renowned for its stunning beaches, vibrant marine life, and rich cultural heritage. Historically inhabited by indigenous tribes, Panglao has a storied past dating back to pre-colonial times, with archaeological evidence suggesting early settlements dating as far back as the Neolithic period. Over the centuries, Panglao has been influenced by various cultures, including the Spanish, who arrived in the Philippines in the 16th century and established Catholic missions on the island.

What to do: Panglao Island offers an array of activities and attractions to suit every traveler's taste. Visitors can spend their days lounging on pristine white sand beaches, swimming in crystal-clear waters, and exploring vibrant coral reefs teeming with marine life. Snorkeling and diving enthusiasts can discover the underwater wonders of sites like Balicasag Island and Pamilacan Island, renowned for their diverse marine ecosystems and colorful coral gardens. Panglao also boasts natural attractions such as the Hinagdanan Cave, a limestone cave with a natural pool, and the scenic Alona Beach, known for its bustling nightlife and beachfront restaurants.

Food to eat: Panglao Island is a paradise for food lovers, offering a wide range of culinary delights to satisfy every palate. Visitors can indulge in fresh seafood dishes, including grilled fish, squid adobo, and shrimp kinilaw, prepared using locally sourced ingredients. For those craving international flavors, Panglao's dining scene features a variety of restaurants serving Asian, European, and American cuisine. Be sure to try traditional Filipino dishes such as lechon (roast pig), pancit (noodles), and halo-halo (a shaved ice dessert).

Transportation: Panglao Island is accessible by air and sea, with regular flights and ferry services connecting the island to major cities in the Philippines. The island is served by Panglao International Airport, located in the municipality of Panglao, which offers domestic and international flights to and from destinations such as Manila, Cebu, and Seoul. Additionally, ferry services operate between Panglao and nearby islands, providing convenient transportation for travelers exploring the region by sea. Once on the island, visitors can get around by rental car, motorcycle, tricycle, or bicycle, with taxis and ride-hailing services also available for transportation within Panglao.





Tagbilaran

Origin/History: Tagbilaran City, the capital of Bohol province, has a rich history dating back to pre-colonial times. The city's name is said to have originated from the word "Tagubilaan," meaning to look out for an enemy in the local dialect. Tagbilaran was historically inhabited by indigenous tribes, including the Boholanos, who established settlements along the coast and engaged in trade with neighboring islands. In the 16th century, Spanish colonizers arrived in the Philippines and established Tagbilaran as a strategic outpost for their missionary and administrative activities. Over the centuries, Tagbilaran evolved into a bustling commercial center and cultural hub, influenced by Spanish, American, and Asian cultures.

What to do: Tagbilaran City offers visitors a blend of historical landmarks, cultural attractions, and modern amenities to explore. History enthusiasts can visit sites such as the Blood Compact Shrine, commemorating the historic blood compact between Spanish explorer Miguel López de Legazpi and Bohol chieftain Datu Sikatuna in 1565, which symbolized friendship and alliance. The Bohol Provincial Capitol Complex, with its elegant colonial-era architecture, is another notable landmark worth visiting. For those interested in arts and culture, the Bohol Provincial Museum showcases exhibits on the island's history, culture, and natural heritage.

Food to eat: Tagbilaran City boasts a diverse culinary scene, offering a mix of local delicacies and international cuisine. Visitors can savor traditional Boholano dishes such as binagol (sticky rice cake), calamay (sweet rice pudding), and sikwate (local hot chocolate) at local eateries and street food stalls. Seafood lovers can indulge in fresh catches from the nearby waters, including grilled fish, shrimp, and squid served with rice and dipping sauces. For a taste of international flavors, Tagbilaran's restaurants and cafes offer a variety of dishes influenced by Spanish, American, Chinese, and Filipino cuisines.

Transportation: Tagbilaran City is a transportation hub in Bohol, with various options for getting around the city and exploring the surrounding areas. The city is served by Tagbilaran City Port, which connects Bohol to neighboring islands such as Cebu and Leyte via ferry services. Tagbilaran also has a domestic airport, Tagbilaran Airport, offering flights to and from destinations such as Manila, Cebu, and Davao. Within the city, visitors can travel by tricycle, jeepney, or taxi, with rental cars and motorcycles also available for exploring the city and its outskirts. Additionally, organized tours and transportation services are offered by hotels and travel agencies for sightseeing and excursions to popular attractions in Tagbilaran and beyond.





Tubigon

Origin/History: Tubigon is a coastal municipality located on the northwestern part of Bohol island. The town traces its origins back to pre-colonial times when it was inhabited by indigenous tribes known as the Pintados. During the Spanish colonial period, Tubigon served as a vital port and trading center for agricultural products and commodities. Over the years, Tubigon grew into a bustling town with a diverse population engaged in fishing, farming, and commerce. Today, Tubigon remains an important gateway to neighboring islands, with its port serving as a major transportation hub for passengers and cargo traveling between Bohol, Cebu, and other destinations in the Visayas region.

What to do: Tubigon offers visitors a range of activities and attractions to enjoy, from exploring scenic landscapes to immersing in local culture. Nature enthusiasts can embark on island-hopping tours to nearby islets and sandbars, where they can swim, snorkel, and sunbathe amidst pristine coastal scenery. Tubigon's mangrove forests and wetlands are ideal for birdwatching and eco-tourism, providing opportunities to spot a variety of avian species in their natural habitat. History buffs can visit Tubigon's historic landmarks, such as the Tubigon Church (St. Isidore the Farmer Parish Church), which dates back to the Spanish colonial era and showcases architectural features influenced by Baroque and Neoclassical styles.

Food to eat: Tubigon offers a taste of authentic Boholano cuisine, featuring dishes made from locally sourced ingredients and fresh seafood caught from nearby waters. Visitors can sample traditional delicacies such as kinilaw (ceviche), sinugbang isda (grilled fish), and paksiw na isda (fish stewed in vinegar and spices) at local eateries and seafood restaurants. For a quick snack or refreshment, travelers can try street food specialties like bibingka (rice cake), kakanin (rice delicacies), and halo-halo (shaved ice dessert) from vendors and stalls around Tubigon's public market and town center.

Transportation: Tubigon is easily accessible by land, sea, and air, with various transportation options available for travelers to reach the town and explore its surroundings. The town is connected to neighboring municipalities and cities via a network of well-maintained roads and highways, making it accessible by car, bus, or motorcycle. Tubigon Port serves as the main gateway for ferry services linking Bohol to Cebu and other islands in the Visayas region, providing daily trips for passengers and vehicles. Additionally, Tubigon has a domestic airport, Tubigon Airport, offering flights to and from select destinations in the Philippines, providing convenient access for air travelers visiting the town and its attractions.





Philippine Wildlife Sanctuary Corella,Bohol

Origin/History: The Philippine Tarsier Sanctuary in Corella, Bohol, is a testament to the conservation efforts aimed at protecting the critically endangered Philippine tarsier (Tarsius syrichta), one of the smallest primates in the world. Established in response to the declining population of tarsiers due to habitat loss and poaching, the sanctuary serves as a protected area where these elusive creatures can thrive in their natural habitat. Corella's commitment to wildlife conservation dates back to the early 1990s when local authorities, together with environmental organizations and concerned citizens, launched initiatives to safeguard the tarsiers and their forest habitat.

What to do: Visiting the Philippine Tarsier Sanctuary in Corella offers travelers a unique opportunity to observe these fascinating creatures up close while learning about their biology, behavior, and conservation status. Guided tours led by knowledgeable park rangers provide insights into the tarsiers' nocturnal lifestyle and specialized adaptations, allowing visitors to appreciate the importance of preserving their forest habitat. Along the sanctuary's trails, visitors can encounter other native wildlife species, including birds, reptiles, and insects, while enjoying the tranquility of the surrounding forest ecosystem. Educational programs and interpretive exhibits at the sanctuary's visitor center offer additional learning experiences for visitors of all ages, highlighting the importance of biodiversity conservation and sustainable ecotourism practices.

Food to eat: While the Philippine Tarsier Sanctuary does not have on-site dining facilities, visitors can explore nearby restaurants and eateries in Corella and neighboring towns to sample local delicacies and refreshments. Bohol is known for its rich culinary heritage, featuring a variety of dishes made from fresh seafood, tropical fruits, and locally sourced ingredients. Travelers can savor traditional Boholano specialties such as kinilaw (ceviche), sinugbang isda (grilled fish), and adobo sa gata (chicken or pork stewed in coconut milk) at restaurants and food stalls in Corella and surrounding communities.

Transportation: Corella is conveniently located within a short distance from popular tourist destinations in Bohol, making it accessible by various modes of transportation. Visitors can reach the Philippine Tarsier Sanctuary by private car, motorcycle, or hired transportation services from Tagbilaran City, the provincial capital of Bohol, which is approximately 10 kilometers away. Public buses, vans, and tricycles also provide transportation options for travelers exploring the island. Once in Corella, visitors can easily navigate the local area on foot or by renting bicycles or motorcycles to reach the sanctuary and other points of interest.





Sikatuna’s Mirror of the World and Botanical Garden

Origin/History: Sikatuna's Mirror of the World and Botanical Garden is a picturesque destination nestled in the heart of Bohol, Philippines. Established as a haven for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts, the botanical garden traces its roots to local conservation efforts aimed at preserving the region's rich biodiversity while providing visitors with a serene retreat amidst lush greenery. Named after Datu Sikatuna, a revered chieftain in Philippine history known for his legendary pact of friendship with Spanish explorer Miguel López de Legazpi, the garden embodies the spirit of harmony between man and nature.

What to do: Exploring Sikatuna's Mirror of the World and Botanical Garden offers visitors a multifaceted experience blending natural beauty, cultural heritage, and recreational activities. Stroll along scenic pathways winding through verdant gardens adorned with a diverse array of indigenous plants, colorful flowers, and towering trees, each contributing to the garden's vibrant tapestry of flora. Discover tranquil ponds, cascading waterfalls, and reflective mirrors strategically placed throughout the landscape, creating captivating vistas and opportunities for contemplation.

The garden's botanical collections showcase a wide variety of plant species native to Bohol and the Philippines, providing educational insights into the region's unique ecosystems and ecological importance. Visitors can participate in guided tours led by knowledgeable botanists and horticulturists, offering fascinating interpretations of the garden's botanical wonders and conservation initiatives. For outdoor enthusiasts, Sikatuna's Mirror of the World and Botanical Garden offers opportunities for leisurely picnics, birdwatching, and photography amidst the natural splendor of the surroundings. Families can enjoy interactive exhibits, educational workshops, and recreational facilities catering to visitors of all ages, fostering a deeper appreciation for nature and environmental stewardship.

Food to eat: While Sikatuna's Mirror of the World and Botanical Garden does not feature on-site dining establishments, visitors can explore nearby towns and villages to sample local cuisine and culinary delights. Bohol is renowned for its gastronomic offerings, featuring a diverse range of dishes influenced by Spanish, Asian, and indigenous flavors. Travelers can indulge in regional specialties such as seafood kinilaw (ceviche), chicken inasal (grilled chicken), and crispy pata (deep-fried pork knuckle) at restaurants, eateries, and food stalls in Sikatuna and neighboring communities.

Transportation: Sikatuna is conveniently located within a short distance from major towns and tourist destinations in Bohol, accessible by various modes of transportation. Visitors can reach Sikatuna's Mirror of the World and Botanical Garden by private car, motorcycle, or hired transportation services from Tagbilaran City, the provincial capital of Bohol, which is approximately 40 kilometers away. Public buses, vans, and tricycles also provide transportation options for travelers exploring the island. Once in Sikatuna, visitors can easily navigate the local area on foot or by renting bicycles or motorcycles to explore the garden and nearby attractions.





TSEDI VIEW PARK

Origin/History:Tsedi View Park, nestled in the municipality of Mabini, Bohol, Philippines, is a picturesque destination offering panoramic views of the surrounding landscapes. Named after the local term "Tsedi," which means "to look from a high place," the park provides visitors with an elevated vantage point to admire the scenic beauty of Bohol.

What to do:At Tsedi View Park, visitors can partake in various activities to fully appreciate its natural splendor. They can embark on leisurely strolls along the park's trails while marveling at the breathtaking vistas of the countryside, including rolling hills and verdant landscapes. Photography enthusiasts will find ample opportunities to capture stunning images, particularly during sunrise or sunset. Additionally, the park features designated viewing areas and seating spots where guests can relax and immerse themselves in the tranquil ambiance.

Food to eat:While Tsedi View Park does not have on-site dining facilities, visitors are welcome to bring their own snacks and refreshments to enjoy a picnic amidst the captivating scenery. Alternatively, they can explore nearby eateries in Mabini town, where they can sample local delicacies and fresh seafood dishes.

Transportation:Tsedi View Park is easily accessible by various modes of transportation. Visitors can travel to Mabini town by private car, rented motorcycle, or public transportation such as buses or vans. From Mabini, it's a short journey to the park's entrance, where ample parking spaces are available for vehicles. Tourists can also opt for organized tours that include visits to Tsedi View Park as part of their itinerary.
